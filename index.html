About Us

Mission Statement

To lead the food festival scene by offering global food trends in a way that respects the environment as we reduce, reuse and recycle in every event. To support the community by hiring local employees and talents with a focus on equal opportunity. To make every IFFI event support the effort to bring clean drinking water to remote areas in the Philippines.

Purpose

To offer a new culinary experience to the market as they gather communities and let them unwind through superb food and music.

Vision

To be the preferred food festival organizer offering tasteful delicacies from different places and great, relaxing music that will be enjoyed by the community.

Core Values

IFFI believes in Customer Orientation and will always prioritize the customer’s needs and wants. The company believes in Teamwork knowing that the best results are accomplished when things are done together. IFFI also upholds Integrity & Excellence believing that honesty and giving their best to the clients will win their trust and make IFFI their preferred organizer.

Goals

To become the brand name to reckon with when it comes to food festival and entertainment through our marketing efforts and excellent service. To make every IFFI location a hub for food, with people who passionately prepare food and for music enthusiasts, catering to those who love to eat their hearts out while enjoying and relaxing to enlivening and soothing music.

Upcoming Festivals

Boracay

Boracay is a small island in the Philippines located in Aklan, Province. Aside from its white sand beaches, Boracay is also famous for being one of the world’s top destinations for relaxation. It is also emerging among the top destinations for tranquillity and nightlife. Boracay island is a fast becoming little known tourist secret. The food variety on the island is very diverse. You name it, you can probably find it. There is of course local food, but also a lot of international cuisine like Italian, Spanish, Chinese etc. But with IFFI Food Festival arriving in Boracay soon, you’ll definitely have the complete vacation you’ve ever had!

Manila

Manila, the capital of the Philippines, is a bustling city in Southeast Asia. Recently reported as one of Asia’s fastest growing economies right after China. The Philippines is enjoying a growing employment rate as well as a soaring BPO ( Business Process Outsourcing) industry. The Local Filipino Food is not what you would expect in Southeast Asia, it is less filled with vegetables and more focused on meat. Filipinos love to grab a bite on the go. A street scene in Manila is not complete without a street food vendor. Here in IFFI Food Festival we present to you some of the craziest and/or most delicious of uniquely Filipino food & snacks.

Cebu

Cebu in particular is considered the second economic power in the Philippines, next to Manila. It is a significant culture centre in the Philippines. The city’s most famous landmark is Magellan’s Cross. Cebu has certainly grown to become a strong financial Hub. The rising tourism in Cebu and the best services, particularly those in the Food and Beverage industry is gradually catching up with the nations capital in terms of economic growth. Here with IFFI Food Festival would be launching the very first Food Festival in the main city of Visayas.

Palawan

Palawan is an island province of the Philippines that is located in the Mimaroparegion. It is the largest province in the country in terms of total area of jurisdiction. Its capital is the well known Puerto Princesa City. Palawan was hailed as world’s best island by the Travel + Leizure Magazine. Readers ranked the Island according to their activities and sights, natural attractions and beaches. Exotic Food is the best recipe serving in Palawan particularly the Crocodile Sisig, a variation of the sizzling dish that typically uses pork. To have a more memorable dining experience, IFFI Food Festival will be coming over to serve you more fun and exciting various tastes of foods & snacks.

Quezon City

Travelling doesn’t necessarily mean going out to far-flung places. If anything, it’s all about seeking out for a new experience and trying out new activities. It’s a good thing that Quezon City is never a boring suburb. The city has nine tourism districts, these districts are Civic Center District.making it a growing and favourite urban travel destination among tourists. It is called “The city of the Stars”. So if you want a place where you can appreciate art, splurge on shopping, or go restaurant-hopping, you can never run out of a place to go to Quezon City. IFFI Food Festival is proudly to announce that we will be showcasing Foods & Snacks as well with Quezon City’s continuous commercial development.

Davao

It’s more fun in the Philippines. Davao is well-known city aside from it’s where the new President of the Philippines came from, Davao city is a the hub of commerce and industry in the Southern Mindanao region. Davao City continuous to straddle two worlds – the new and the old. A favourite destination for study tours, Davao City is regarded as a model for other cities in the country. When in Davao City, you enjoy the freshest and highest quality agricultural produce. Davao City cannot be measured when it comes to eateries, it is so because the Dabawenyos’ humble way of daily life dictates their simple tastes and choices of food. Naturally, the capital city is a superlative place to dine where you are offered with choices of foods available at IFFI Food Festival where will have a different cuisines like American hamburgers and french fried potatoes, Chinese cuisines, Korean food and Japanese tastes are able to satiate your hunger.

Makati

Makati Is one of the well-known shopping hubs of Metro Manila. The center is known for its wide array of shopping, entertainment and cultural offerings surrounded by BPO and Commercial establishments. The premier shopping centers, offering both International and local retail shops, high-end boutique, dining outlets and entertainment facilities can be found around the city. IFFI Food festival is delighted to be part of the city to serve International and Local various food that offers with affordable prices.

Angeles, Clark Pampanga

Clark is a sprawling, cosmopolitan metropolis that offers many places of interests and activities accessible to a wide spectrum of tourists and visitors. Located in the heart of Philippine’s Central Luzon region, Clark is part of Pampanga province. With a highly improved road network and increasing flights at CIAC, Clark is setting out to become the hub for business, aviation and tourism in the Philippines. Whether for business or leisure numerous choices await even the most discriminating business traveller or visitor, Duty-free shopping and fine dining to unwind after a hard day’s work? Choose from a variety of foods and snacks at IFFI Food Festival and have some relaxing moment with soft music played with our acoustic bands.
